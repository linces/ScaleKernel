# Canonical Definitions

## Kernel

The invariant conceptual architecture shared across all specializations.

The Kernel describes relationships.

Never implementations.

---

## Constraint

Any condition that limits, shapes or governs the manifestation of the Kernel.

Constraints explain diversity.

They do not require different Kernels.

---

## Specialization

The observable realization of the Kernel under a particular set of Constraints.

Every domain-specific system is interpreted as a specialization.

---

## Interaction

Any event through which elements influence one another.

Every interaction changes system state.

Interaction is the source of transformation.

---

## Transformation

The process through which interactions modify system state.

Transformation currently represents one of the deepest known concepts within the framework.

Its relationship with information and energy remains an open research topic.

---

## Information

Canonical definition:

> **Information is any persistent influence capable of affecting future system behavior.**

Information is defined functionally.

Not technologically.

Not physically.

Not symbolically.

---

## Feedback

The recursive influence of previous system states upon future interactions.

Feedback enables self-regulation.

---

## Adaptation

The emergent consequence of recursive feedback operating under constraints.

Adaptation is never assumed.

It emerges.

---

## Emergence

The spontaneous appearance of higher-order behavior resulting from repeated local interactions.

No centralized coordination is required.

---

## Structural Stability

The ability of a system to preserve its organizational identity while continuously changing its behavior.

Stability concerns architecture.

Not immobility.

---

## Elegance

Elegance is an emergent architectural quality.

Current research indicates that elegance arises from the combination of:

* simplicity;
* structural stability;
* conceptual coherence;
* efficiency;
* low coupling;
* high cohesion;
* adaptability;
* explanatory power;
* economy of principles.

No single property defines elegance.
